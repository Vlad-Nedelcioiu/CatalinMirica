## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment: fails the "which AI made this?" test at the composition level, despite above-average within-section craft.** The skeleton is the modal AI landing page: brass tracked-caps eyebrow on **7 of 8 sections** (5 of them dropping the sanctioned dash+label format DESIGN.md requires), an animated count-up stat band (`Stats.tsx` — the banned hero-metric template, running placeholder numbers PRODUCT.md says must never ship), a 4-up identical icon-card services grid (`Services.tsx:29–50` — banned identical card grid + rounded icon plates above headings), an auto-rotating testimonial carousel, and a dark rounded CTA panel — stacked in exactly the template order the "generic template look" anti-reference forbids. Clean: no side-stripes, no gradient text, no gratuitous glass; Process's 01–04 numbers are earned (a real sequence). Cream + Playfair + brass is the first-order category reflex, but it's committed brand identity now — identity-preservation wins; the composition, not the palette, is the problem.

**Deterministic scan: 2 advisory findings, both `design-system-font-size`** — `text-[0.6rem]` in the Navbar logo descriptor (`Navbar.tsx:60`) and `md:text-[2.75rem]` in the shared `SectionHeading` (`Section.tsx:38`). Both confirmed genuine (real rendered markup, off the DESIGN.md type ramp); the Section one is higher-leverage since every section heading inherits it. Notably the detector's rule set can't see the compositional tells the design review caught — the two assessments agree the primitives are clean and disagree only in altitude.

**Visual overlays**: not available — no dev server was running, no playwright/puppeteer installed, no native browser tool exposed; the browser leg was skipped with that fallback signal recorded. No user-visible overlay exists.

## Overall Impression

A genuinely coherent design system executing a generic composition. The primitives (tokens, borders, elevation discipline, button system, shared section components) are real and consistently obeyed — rare — but the page assembles them into the 2024–26 template scroll, undermining design principle #2 ("the site is itself the proof") for the exact design-literate audience the studio sells to. The single biggest opportunity: let the photography lead — move the work up, cut the scaffolding sections, and make Services photographic. The second biggest: the ending violates the brand ("Dates book quickly during peak season" urgency on a dark panel) at the precise peak-end moment that sets the lasting impression.

## What's Working

1. **The system primitives are real and obeyed.** One accent, 1px deckle borders, shared `Section`/`Reveal`/`buttonClasses` primitives, flat-at-rest elevation with hover-only lift. Coherent codebase-level system, not per-section improvisation.
2. **FeaturedWork's asymmetric grid** (7/5 then 4/4/4, varied heights) breaks the identical-card reflex and gives the photography editorial pacing; the category/title/location caption stacks read like album captions.
3. **The copy voice mostly lands the brand.** "From hello to heirloom," "a friend with an exceptional eye in the room," and testimonials that sound like people deliver "warmth without sentimentality" better than the layout does.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Mobile hero grid has two empty holes — the first screen looks broken on phones**
- **Why it matters**: leads arrive warm from Instagram, overwhelmingly on phones; the first impression on the majority device is a layout with visible cream voids. `Hero.tsx:61` uses `grid-cols-2` below `lg` with 1/2/1-span children in source order, so sparse auto-placement leaves empty cells at row 1 col 2 and row 3 col 2.
- **Fix**: reorder for mobile — main image first at `col-span-2`, then a/b as a two-up row; restore the triptych with `lg:order-*`. (`Hero.tsx:61–93`)
- **Suggested command**: /impeccable adapt

**[P1] Conversion hierarchy inverts the documented strategy, with four labels for one destination**
- **Why it matters**: PRODUCT.md's primary CTA is "View portfolio" (the work does the selling), but the hero fills "Book a session" and outlines the portfolio (`Hero.tsx:44–53`). `/booking` is reached via "Book a Session" (`Navbar.tsx:83`), "Book a session" (Hero), "See packages" (`Services.tsx:23` — promises pricing, delivers a booking flow), and "Check availability" (`CTASection.tsx:37`, `Footer.tsx:88`). Broken information scent + salesman-first ordering the brand explicitly rejects.
- **Fix**: swap hero button hierarchy; standardize on one booking label; make "See packages" deep-link to packages or rename it.
- **Suggested command**: /impeccable clarify

**[P1] WCAG AA contrast failures on brass and muted text — against an explicit AA commitment**
- **Why it matters**: computed ratios: brass `#a9743f` eyebrows 3.74:1 on cream / 3.99:1 on paper (needs 4.5:1, used 7×); muted `#8b857a` hero trust row 3.43:1 (`Hero.tsx:107–109`); footer legal row 3.23:1; testimonial roles, "Est. in SF", navbar descriptor all fail; placeholder `text-muted/80` on paper 2.69:1 (`Field.tsx:5`, sitewide). These are real content, not decoration.
- **Fix**: add a text-safe brass step (e.g. `#8a5a2b` = 5.49:1 on cream) for small brass text; move failing muted text to `text-ink-soft` (8.63:1); darken placeholders.
- **Suggested command**: /impeccable audit

**[P1] The reduced-motion promise is not kept, and content is gated at opacity 0**
- **Why it matters**: the global CSS rule only neutralizes CSS animation; Framer Motion (every `Reveal`, testimonial crossfade, rAF stat counter) runs at full motion under `prefers-reduced-motion`. And `initial={{opacity: 0}}` server-renders below-fold content invisible — no-JS visitors, reader modes, and headless renderers get a blank page (a named motion ban).
- **Fix**: wrap the app in `<MotionConfig reducedMotion="user">` (`app/layout.tsx`); `useReducedMotion()` in the counter; prefer a CSS-first reveal that's default-visible.
- **Suggested command**: /impeccable audit

**[P2] Template scaffolding: eyebrow saturation, the stat band, the icon-card grid** *(strategically the biggest opportunity)*
- **Why it matters**: 7/8 sections carry the eyebrow (5 in an unsanctioned dash-less format); Stats is the banned hero-metric template on placeholder numbers; Services is the banned identical icon-card grid. A taste-selling studio whose site reads as a template loses the sale at the aesthetic layer.
- **Fix**: keep the full dash+label eyebrow on 2–3 sections and cut the rest; remove/shrink Stats until real numbers exist (fold one true proof point into the hero trust row); re-art-direct Services as photo-led editorial rows — each service deserves a photograph, not a lucide icon.
- **Suggested command**: /impeccable distill (scaffolding) + /impeccable bolder (photo-led Services)

## Minor Observations

- Carousel violates WCAG 2.2.2 (auto-updating >5s, no pause control) and has no `aria-live`, so screen readers never hear quote changes.
- No skip-to-content link in `app/layout.tsx`.
- `rounded-3xl` on `AboutTeaser.tsx:22` and `CTASection.tsx:13` — off the documented radius scale.
- "Est. 2017" floating card carries `shadow-soft` while static — violates the Flat-at-Rest Rule.
- CTA panel: near-viewport dark ink surface drifts toward the "dark moody luxury" anti-reference.
- `twitter.card: summary_large_image` declared with no OG image.
- Work-card hover *darkens* the photo (scrim 80→95%) — inverted affordance for a portfolio.
- Under reduced motion, kenburns' `forwards` fill parks the hero at scale(1.16) — permanently tighter crop.
- "Moments" appears three ways across hero/footer taglines; navbar "Book a Session" vs hero "Book a session" capitalization drift.
- Detector: `text-[0.6rem]` (Navbar) and `md:text-[2.75rem]` (SectionHeading) are off the DESIGN.md type ramp.

## Questions to Consider

1. **If the work does the talking, why does it wait until scroll three?** What would this page be if FeaturedWork sat directly under the hero, Stats disappeared until real numbers exist, and Services were four photographs instead of four lucide icons?
2. **Swap the logo for a competitor's — does anything break?** Right now the only untransferable moments are the Santorini caption pill and one AboutTeaser sentence.
3. **What does the back cover of an heirloom album look like?** The page ends on a dark panel and an urgency line — the one moment where the album metaphor and the no-salesmanship principle both break. Could it end like closing the album: one full-bleed photograph, one quiet line, one unhurried invitation?
